The Recycling Blueprint

The Recycling Blueprint reimagines recycling in student residences through a structured framework inspired by social practice theory, addressing systemic barriers to sustainable behavior. This initiative introduces an integrated approach combining policy changes, behavioral nudges, and user-centric tools to create an environment where recycling becomes second nature. Through an opt-in program, the centralisation of waste and recyclables disposal, and implementation of supportive elements such as personal recycling bags, a recycling flowchart, and real-time progress tracking at the Resource Sorting Stations (RSS), it empowers students to act on their recycling intentions. By reconfiguring the meanings, materials, and skills surrounding residents, the Blueprint builds the optimal environment that will transform recycling into a socially integrated practice that is effortless, rewarding, and impactful, making sustainability a consistent practice in NUS.

INVENTRO

While efficiency in healthcare is prioritised, the fast-paced environment often leads to minimal inventory management efforts, causing supply wastage and impacting sustainability. Designed to cultivate sustained awareness on stock tracking and optimal supply usage, Inventro leverages on visual cues and hierarchy to streamline the ordering, sorting, retrieval, and replenishing routines of healthcare staff, improve their workflow, and ultimately minimise expired supplies within their inventory.
